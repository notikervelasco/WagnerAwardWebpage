Sports Particpant

GOAL! SCORE! GREAT RUN! These are just a few examples of things I've heard throughout my sports journey. I consider myself non-typical in the world of sports, having played around eight different sports from the age of six to the present day. Throughout my life, I’ve continuously participated in one or more sports, and this has shaped me in ways I never expected. I’ve fallen in love with the competition, teamwork, and leadership that sports offer, and I’m excited to share my journey with you.

This photo showcases my first sport: soccer! I’ve played both recreational and competitive soccer since the age of six, and it has always held a special place in my heart. I’ve come to truly appreciate the teamwork, leadership, and challenges that the sport brings, and I will always cherish the experience.
My second sport was gymnastics! Whether it was the bars, the foam pit, or the runway, my memories of gymnastics were brief but fond. I can still do a cartwheel and handstand—which still manages to impress some people! As an individual sport, it taught me valuable skills like personal motivation, resilience, and determination, and it’s an experience that will always stay with me.
My next sport is one I still practice and love to this day: swimming! I started competitive swimming when I was eight and have been in love with the water ever since. As silly as it sounds, I’ve always felt like Moana—drawn to the water—whether it’s the beach, a pool, or the ocean. I'm currently a lifeguard for Milwaukee County, and I have my swimming experience to thank for that.
My next sport was football. I played for 'The Saints,' a Catholic school team outside of my own, and took on the position of wide receiver. While I wasn’t particularly great during the two short years I played, the experience will always stay with me. It taught me what it truly means to be part of a team—and how, through all the different positions, routes, and plays happening at once, something powerful and even beautiful can come together.
High school sports will always have a special place in my heart, and one of my earliest high school memories is from cross country. I ran all four years, eventually competing at state, earning the title of state academic athlete, receiving a four-year varsity letter, and serving as team captain. I’ve learned so much from this sport that it’s hard to capture it all in one short paragraph—this chapter of my life was truly unforgettable.
Last but not least, track and field. I’ve been running track since middle school, always focusing on distance—whether it was the 800, mile, or 2-mile. The challenge of long-distance races, the teammates I’ve met along the way, and the lifelong friendships that formed made track and field a truly one-of-a-kind experience. I know the experiences, lessons, and skills I’ve gained over the years will continue to shape me—not just into a better athlete, but a better man.

Community/Faith

Community and faith have always been central to my identity. Growing up attending a private Catholic school in Milwaukee, I fondly remember attending Friday Mass every week. The scent of the basilica, the sight of hundreds of people coming together to celebrate the same faith—it was a truly transformative experience. I can still picture the local bakers from whom my family would buy goods, the police officers who worked tirelessly to keep our streets safe, and the workers at the local park where we would go for recess. These experiences shaped my understanding of what it means to be part of a community and the role faith plays in bringing people together.

  1. St. Josaphat Basilica Church

    2. As a student at St. Josaphat Basilica School, we attended Mass every Friday, and I was offered full membership to the church as I came of age. This church holds special memories for me. On weekends, it hosts a food pantry for those in need, and I was grateful for the opportunity to volunteer there. Through this experience, I was able to form genuine connections with my neighbors, the staff, and the broader community.

  2. Camp Anokijig

    3. As I got older, I was invited to spend part of my summer volunteering at Camp Anokijig in Plymouth, Wisconsin. As a junior camp counselor, I was able to stay for free in exchange for helping ensure the safety and well-being of kids ages 6 to 14. We were also responsible for making sure they had a great time—which wasn’t hard at such a magical place. Located on a lake, I got to teach sailing, lead water sports, and share my love for the water while passing on skills to others. I spent over six weeks volunteering during the summer of 2022, and I can confidently say it was one of my favorite volunteer experiences.

  3. OMG

    4. OMG—short for the Original Milwaukee Running Group—is the oldest running organization in Milwaukee, and my family and I have been proud members since around 2021. As a member, I not only participate in runs but also volunteer at major events like the Lakefront Marathon. Being part of this community, especially alongside my family, has allowed me to experience Milwaukee in a whole new way. Running through different neighborhoods has helped me truly see and appreciate all that my community has to offer.

Patriotism

One of my earliest memories is of my dad telling me stories from his time stationed in Japan. He would describe the people he met, the culture he experienced, and the rich history of fighters and warriors that Japan is known for. On special occasions, he would even show me the sword he brought back as a souvenir. As a child, I didn’t fully understand the sacrifices he made to serve, protect, and honor our country. But as I’ve grown older, I’ve come to admire his dedication, and it has inspired me to honor my country in my own way.

Patriotism has always been an important part of who I am. I’ve had the honor of singing the national anthem at various events, each time feeling a deep sense of pride and respect for my country. I’ve also been taught the proper way to raise and fold the American flag—traditions that I take seriously and approach with great care. Inspired by my father, who served in the Army, I’ve considered following a similar path. I’m currently exploring the possibility of joining the Army Reserve as a way to serve my country while continuing to grow personally and professionally. For me, patriotism isn’t just a feeling—it’s something I strive to show through action and commitment.
