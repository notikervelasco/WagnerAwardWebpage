Community/Faith

Community and faith have always been central to my identity. Growing up attending a private Catholic school in Milwaukee, I fondly remember attending Friday Mass every week. The scent of the basilica, the sight of hundreds of people coming together to celebrate the same faith—it was a truly transformative experience. I can still picture the local bakers from whom my family would buy goods, the police officers who worked tirelessly to keep our streets safe, and the workers at the local park where we would go for recess. These experiences shaped my understanding of what it means to be part of a community and the role faith plays in bringing people together.

Patriotism

One of my earliest memories is of my dad telling me stories from his time stationed in Japan. He would describe the people he met, the culture he experienced, and the rich history of fighters and warriors that Japan is known for. On special occasions, he would even show me the sword he brought back as a souvenir. As a child, I didn’t fully understand the sacrifices he made to serve, protect, and honor our country. But as I’ve grown older, I’ve come to admire his dedication, and it has inspired me to honor my country in my own way.
